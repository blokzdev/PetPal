# Senior dog basics

A "senior" dog is loosely 7+ for medium-large breeds, 10+ for small
breeds. Senior care is mostly about catching things early and adjusting
expectations, not aggressive intervention.

- **Twice-yearly vet visits**, not annual. Subtle bloodwork shifts
  catch kidney/liver/thyroid changes before symptoms.
- **Watch for cognitive dysfunction.** Pacing at night, getting "lost"
  in familiar rooms, staring at corners, change in toileting patterns.
  Treatable; mention vet consult.
- **Sensory loss is normal but worth noting.** Vision and hearing both
  decline. Don't startle a deaf dog; approach into their visual field.
- **Mobility shifts.** Reduced jumping, hesitation on stairs, taking
  longer to lie down. Could be arthritis (very common) or something
  more serious — log entries make the trend visible.
- **Weight management is critical.** Senior dogs do worst when
  overweight; joints, cardiovascular, all of it. Watch the trend, not
  the snapshot.

Use `write_wiki_entry` for every observed change — senior care is a
trend-tracking job.
