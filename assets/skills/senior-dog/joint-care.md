# Joint care for senior dogs

Arthritis is the single most underdiagnosed senior-dog issue — by the
time a dog visibly limps, it's been compensating for months.

**Early signs the owner might mention:**
- Slower to rise from a lying position
- Reluctance on stairs (especially down)
- Reduced jumping (couch, car) — often blamed on "getting lazy"
- Stiffness after rest, looser after warming up
- Nail growth changes (less wear on one foot = compensating)

**What helps, by evidence base:**
- **Weight.** Even a 5–10% weight reduction meaningfully reduces joint
  load. This is the single highest-leverage intervention.
- **Controlled exercise.** Swimming, leash walks on flat ground,
  gentle hill work. Avoid frisbee, agility-style turns, and prolonged
  high-impact play.
- **Joint diets / fish-oil supplementation.** Modest evidence; useful
  adjunct.
- **NSAIDs.** Effective and safe in healthy dogs with vet
  supervision; contraindicated with kidney disease — bloodwork
  before starting.

**What doesn't help (much):**
- Glucosamine/chondroitin: weak evidence in dogs.
- CBD: very weak evidence; legal patchwork.

If the user describes acute pain or sudden lameness, that's a vet
visit, not a chat suggestion. Defer to escalation copy.
