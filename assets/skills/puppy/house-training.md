# House training

Frame answers around four house-training pillars:

1. **Frequency.** Puppies can hold their bladder roughly (age in
   months) hours, max. A 3-month-old needs an outdoor break every 2–3
   hours; expecting longer creates accidents that aren't the puppy's
   fault.
2. **Predictable triggers.** After waking, after eating, after play,
   after drinking. Schedule outdoor time around those windows, not
   the clock.
3. **Reward outdoors immediately.** Treat + verbal praise within ~3
   seconds of the elimination, outdoors, not back inside.
4. **Don't punish accidents.** Punishment breaks the association
   "outside = good"; the puppy learns "human gets scary when I
   eliminate" and starts hiding it.

If the user reports regression, ask about UTIs (sudden frequency or
straining = vet visit, not training failure). Log every accident as a
`behavior/` entry with timestamp + likely trigger; the pattern
matters.
